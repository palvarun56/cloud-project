What is Cloud Computing?

The Simple Definition

It means using computers over the internet.

Instead of storing files on your own hard drive, you store them on a giant server somewhere else.

Think of it like Electricity

You don't generate your own electricity at home.

You plug into the grid and pay for what you use.

Cloud is the sameâ€”but for computing power.

Where is "The Cloud"?

It's Not in the Sky!

The "Cloud" is actually thousands of physical buildings called Data Centers.

  • They are filled with rows of powerful computers (servers).
  • They run 24/7 to keep your data safe and accessible.
  • They are located all over the world.

Conceptual View: Servers inside a large Facility

Key Features

On-Demand

Get computing power instantly when you need it. No waiting for hardware to arrive.

Scalable

Start small and grow big. Need more storage? Just upgrade your plan instantly.

Accessible

Access your files and apps from any device (Phone, Laptop, Tablet), anywhere.

Types of Cloud Services

1. IaaS (Infrastructure)

Rent the raw hardware (Servers, Storage).
Example: Amazon Web Services (AWS)

2. PaaS (Platform)

Rent tools to build apps.
Example: Google App Engine

3. SaaS (Software)

Rent finished applications.
Example: Gmail, Netflix

SaaS (You Manage Only Data)
PaaS (You Manage Code)
IaaS (You Manage Operating System)

Deployment Models

Public Cloud

Shared by many people (Like a public bus).

Cheap & Easy.

Private Cloud

Dedicated to one company (Like your own car).

Secure & Expensive.

Hybrid Cloud

A mix of both (Like a taxi).

Flexible & Balanced.

You Use Cloud Every Day!

  • Streaming: Netflix streams movies from the cloud, not your TV.
  • Storage: Google Photos keeps your memories safe online.
  • Social Media: Instagram posts are stored on cloud servers.
  • Music: Spotify plays millions of songs without downloading them.

Access from Any Device

Why Switch to Cloud?

Save Money

No need to buy expensive supercomputers. Just pay a small monthly fee.

Data Safety

If you lose your laptop, you don't lose your data. It's safe in the cloud.

Collaboration

Teams can work on the same document at the same time (e.g., Google Docs).

Risks & Challenges

1. Internet Dependency

If your internet goes down, you might lose access to your files.


2. Security & Privacy

You are trusting a third party (like Google or Amazon) to keep your data safe.


3. Cost Management

It's easy to accidentally leave servers running and get a big bill.

Cloud computing is simply renting computing power over the internet.

It makes technology cheaper, faster, and accessible to everyone.
